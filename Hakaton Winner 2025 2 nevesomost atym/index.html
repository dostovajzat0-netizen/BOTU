NBL NASA (Neutral Buoyancy Laboratory)

⸻

The laboratory is a huge pool where astronauts learn to work in conditions as close to zero gravity as possible. Benefits for the World Medicine: Underwater training has led to new methods for rehabilitation after strokes and injuries, such as hydrotherapy and therapeutic pools. Research on underwater breathing is used in the development of equipment for patients with lung diseases. Rescue Technologies: Skills developed in challenging underwater environments help train rescue teams — for example, in flooded mines or during underwater emergencies. Robotics and Prosthetics: Technologies created for underwater and zero-gravity operations have become the foundation for exoskeletons, prosthetics, and medical devices that help people walk again. Science and Education: The NBL serves as a research base for engineers, biomechanical experts, and students. It inspires new generations of scientists and medical professionals. Ocean Technologies: NASA’s experience with underwater training supports the development of safer equipment for deep-sea diving and ocean exploration on Earth. Key Takeaway The NBL is a clear example of how space training directly improves life on Earth — advancing medicine, rescue technologies, and education.
